Analyze for Usability

My thoughts on the usability of Paradise Pet Lodge's website

Posted by Meghan Cahill on April 11, 2020

Paradise Pet Lodge is a daycare/boarding facility for dogs and cats. The URL for the website is https://www.paradisepetlodge.comThe purpose of the page is to provide information about the daycare and boarding services offered by the facility.

Does the page use conventions?

They use some conventions like the placement of the logo on the top left and having a menu bar along the top. They also use the social media logos making it clear that you can access them through Facebook and Instagram and Yelp.

Does the site use effective visual hierarchies?

The site does a good job creating hierarchies through the use of the menu. For each main topic, there is a dropdown menu that breaks up the subtopics. They use different size fonts and colors to distinguish the main topic of the page with the subtopics.

Does the page have a lot of distractions?

The page is organized by breaking up topics into clearly defined sections by using different size font, color, and dotted lines to distinguish each section. However, the text is very small making it hard to read and scan. Between the compact text and the pictures, there is a lot of information to scan.

Does It omit useless words?

No. There’s a lot of “happy talk” like describing how they have upgraded over the last 30 years which I don’t find that useful.

Suggestions to the website

They should break out the sections into different pages allowing them to make the pictures and text larger as well as looking more organized. They could also use bullets to reduce the density of the text and eliminate the “happy talk.” This would make it more scannable.
