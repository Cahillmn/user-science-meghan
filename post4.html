Usability Test

A usability test of the Hertz website

Posted by Meghan Cahill on May 2, 2020

Hertz is one of the largest car rental companies in the world. It has over 10,000 corporate and franchisee locations. Last week, I tested the usability of the hertz.com website on a computer. This week, a usability test was conducted to determine the ease of use of the website on mobile devices https://www.hertz.com for older adults. This blog provides a summary of this test and its results.

Summary of the test

Purpose and methods used

The purpose of this test was to test the Hertz website on a mobile device to see how user friendly it is for older adults. The test was conducted on April 30 through May 2, 2020 via Zoom sessions due to the Covid-19 outbreak. Sessions were recorded with the users’ permission.

Each user was asked to log onto the site on a mobile device and I read a script describing that we were testing the usability of the Hertz website. Each user was asked if they had ever used a website to rent a car, and if they had used the Hertz website. The users were asked to perform three tasks and were told to say their thoughts out loud as they performed the tasks. The following tasks were included in the test:

  • Find Hertz locations near you
  • Assume you are making a one-and-a-half-week trip to Seattle. Find a car that meets your needs and determine how much the rental would cost.
  • Find out what offers are available for AAA members.

After the test, I looked back at the videos and noted how much time and the number of actions performed on each task. The full test script can be found here.

Number and demographic summary of participants

In all, there were three participants. Two-thirds identified as male and the average age was 64.7. Two-thirds had previously used a website to rent a car, and the same users had used the Hertz website before. One of those users said it had been a significant amount of time since using the Hertz website.

Results summary

Task 1

In the first task, they were asked to find Hertz locations near them.

The following charts presents the number of steps and time it took the users to complete the task.

Like my previous test using a computer, users gravitated to the reservation functionality to find locations even though the locations feature was a menu item.

Each user approached this task in different ways. Using the menu, user 1 quickly found locations in 32 seconds and four steps.

User 2 spent almost ten minutes trying to find a location using the pickup locations functionality in the reservation section of the home page. They tried using the names of local cities and zip codes which brought up either no results or locations not near their home. At one point, it displayed locations in other states and foreign countries. They felt like the location feature wasn’t done well and that it was surprisingly difficult. They mentioned that they would likely go to another rental car company because of the poor performance of this feature.

Also using the pickup feature, user 3 was able to find a location near their home and finish the task in just over two minutes and nine steps. However, their results were limited to a single location and they didn’t receive information about other locations near them

Task 2

In the second task, the users were asked to make a week and a half reservation and figure how much it would cost.

The following charts presents the number of steps and time it took the users to complete the task.

The experience of people in task two was also very similar to using a computer. Because the reservation feature is so prominent on the home page, they were automatically focused on it.

Users one and three had a similar experience finishing the task in about two and a half minutes. User two took almost nine minutes to complete the task due to difficulty finding a location. Both users one and two noted that you couldn’t clear the reservation information to allow the user to start over. This made the more cumbersome to use. In the end, all the users thought it was a reasonable experience.

Task 3

In task three, the users were told to find what offers were available for AAA members.

The following charts presents the number of steps and time it took the users to complete the task.

User three had no difficulty with this task. They found AAA deals in eighteen seconds and three steps. User one, initially tried to find the information using the menu but couldn’t locate anything about AAA. They then tried the search feature but didn’t find the results they were looking for. They went back to the menu and found deals under the “Rent” sub-menu. Oddly when they returned to the main menu, “Deals” showed up as an option. User three tried the menu but didn’t find anything about deals so they used the search feature three times using different criteria and didn’t find what they were looking for and gave up after about four minutes and eighteen steps.

Summary of issues found and next steps

Issues found

The experience was similar to the prior group who used computers. For both task one and three, the users on both types of devices gravitated towards the reservation section instead of the menu. In general, they all recognized the hamburger icon as the menu; however, they rarely went to it making it harder to find things.

Unlike on the computer, the deals option didn’t show up in the menu unless they clicked on the “Rent” submenu first. Because of the difficulty in finding the menu option for “Deals” users spent a lot of time searching for AAA deals.

Suggested solutions and next steps

As in the prior test, I would recommend a different use of color due to a very bland light on light website. This might help balance the reservation section with the menu and other options on the site.

I would also add some features like a clear button on the reservation section to make it easier for users to start over. The other thing would be to put a hyperlink in the reservations section for locations near me. That would highlight that the feature exists without having to use the menu.
