Just Add Analytics

Using Google Analytics to evaluate my website

Posted by Meghan Cahill on May 23, 2020

Introduction

This week I added Google Analytics to my User Science Journal website to gather data about usage on my site. I wanted to evaluate the following assumptions:

  1. Ten people would visit my site
  2. Less than 10% would return a second time
  3. Users will use the site the most in the evening
  4. At least 75% of people would use a mobile device
  5. My latest blog would be the most popular page

Methods

I loaded Google Analytics using instructions found on the Google Analytics Help Center . In order to increase traffic on my site, I contacted family and asked that they visit my site. At the end of the week I reviewed the data collected to evaluate my assumptions.

Findings

Number of users using my site

I had significantly more users than I expected. Over 43 sessions there were 25 users with a bounce rate of 9.3%. That tells me that most of the users that came to my page were browsing throughout the site. The vast majority accessed my page by directly entering the hyperlink that I provided.

Users were spread geographically across several states in the U.S. They were mostly located on the East coast although the Bellevue area had the greatest number of users.

Return rate of the site

Approximately 27% of users returned to my site which was more than I had anticipated. This tells me that you can always count on family!

Time of day users used the site

My initial assumption that most people would use my site in the evening, turned out to be false. 67% used it before noon, 21% used it between noon and 5:00 pm and only 13% used it 6:00 pm or after. I suspected that people have more spare time in the evening so I was surprised to see that they used it the most in the morning.

Type of device users use to access the site most

I assumed that most people would use a mobile device to view my website. I thought they would see the email on their phone and then go straight to the link. However, most people used a desktop device (over 54%).

Most popular pages in the site

Without considering the home page, I thought that my latest blog post six would be the most popular since that one is listed at the top of the page. Obviously, the home page was the most popular because people have to navigate through it to get to the blogs. Of the blog pages my post 6 was the most popular with 41 page views.

Conclusion

Using Google Analytics was fun and I especially enjoyed seeing people in real time, where they were from and what parts of the site they were viewing. It is very helpful to have data in order to know whether your website is matching your expectations. It allows you to make changes as needed to make your page meet your goals.
