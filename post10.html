Goal Experiment Summary Report

Posted by Meghan Cahill on June 13, 2020

This is the last week of my blog and I will be summarizing my experience creating goals and content experiments using Google Analytics and Optimize.

About my goal

In week 8, I created goals to analyze usage of my website. I set up duration goals of 30 seconds, one minute, and five minutes to track how long users stayed on my blog. For more information about these goals and the information, refer to my week 8 blog

I found that over 60% of users spent five minutes or more on my blog and 87% spent a minute or more.

About my Content Experiment

Setting up my Content Experiment

In week 9 , I summarized setting up my content experiment and how that process went. My content experiment was a Call To Action (CTA) where I changed the subheading on my homepage to encourage people to check back weekly and spend time on my site. I created an A/B test in Optimize and sent users to two versions of the site.

Results of the experiment

This week, the number of users to my site fell to 12 compared to 25 users in week 7. Overall the use of my site has gone down since I stopped calling the clan to action.

Despite the drop in the number of users, I still had 56% of users spend five minutes or more on my page. 81% spent one minute or more. comparing the goal completions with those from last week there wasn’t a significant drop in goal completions.

The results of my content experiment were inconclusive since I didn’t have enough experiments each day.

Of the experiment sessions I did have, it doesn’t appear that my change made any difference. I had three experiment sessions on variant 1 and seven on my original page. The conversion rates on my original page are higher than those on variant 1.

Next steps

If I was continuing my blog, I could add an email feature that would allow users to sign up for alerts from me that might drive up my usage and get more experiments. I could also add more visual effects like videos to hold user’s interest longer in my blog.

Google Analytics

Overall, I thought Google Analytics and Optimize were very useful in collecting data. It is important for web developers to learn more about how users access and use their sites. It allows developers to make changes that they deem necessary based on actual data from users. Google Analytics was easier to understand than Optimize.
